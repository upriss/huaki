Mandolin Sheet Music

The composers on this page wrote original works for mandoline ensembles. Some also arranged music for mandoline ensembles. Further compositions arranged for mandoline ensembles can be found here.

For mandolin quartet/orchestra
  • Giuseppe Anelli Trigolo (1876 - 1926)
    • Risorgimento Italiano (score)
    • Sinfonia Romantica (score)

  • Giuseppe Bellenghi (1847 - 1902) also used a pseudonym: G. B. Pirani
    • Il Solitario (parts) An arrangement of the song "Verlassen bin i" by Thomas Koschat
    • Ricordo d'Italia (score and parts edited by U. Priss)

  • Calogero Adolfo Bracco (1860 - 1905)
    • I mandolini a congresso (score)

  • Alfonso Cipollone (1843 - 1926)
    • Barcarola (parts)
    • Doux Souvenir (parts)
    • Serenata Spagnola (parts)

  • Domenico De Giovanni (1844 - 1925)
    • Flores Andinas (parts)
    • Sinfonia in Sol (score)

  • Willy Fahr (Professor and director of the conservatory in Wiesbaden, disappeared from the address book in 1931) arranged by Fahr:
    • Beethoven: Menuett in G-dur (parts)

  • Carl Henze (1872 - 1946)
    • Paraphrase über Die Königskinder (parts)
    • Am Mühlengrund (score and parts)
    arranged by Henze:
    • Tekla Badarzewska: Maiden's Prayer (parts)
    • Boccherini: Menuett (parts)
    • Haydn: Andante from the Surprise Symphony (parts)
    • Mozart: Menuett from Don Juan (parts)

  • Ferdinand Kollmaneck (1871 - 1941)
    • Kleines Konzert Nr 2 (parts) (Here is a video of the Braunschweig Mandolin Orchestra playing this piece.)
    • Entschwundenes Glück (parts)
    • Weihnachtsliederkranz (score)
    arranged by Kollmaneck:
    • Beethoven: Die Himmel rühmen (score)
    • Richard Eilenberg (1848-1927): Petersburger Schlittenfahrt (score)
    • Haydn: Menuett from the symphony in D major (score)
    • Strauss: An der schönen blauen Donau (parts)
    • Strauss: Frühlingsstimmen Walzer (parts)
    • Strauss: Schatzwalzer (parts)
    • Further works by Kollmaneck are available here.

  • Angelo Liprandi (1848 - 1906)
    • Ouverture (score and parts)

  • Theodor Ritter1 (1883 - 1950)
    • Amazonenritt (parts)
    • Marionetten (parts)
    arranged by Ritter:
    • Brahms: Hungarian Dance Nr 5 (parts)
    • Brahms: Hungarian Dance Nr 6 (parts)
    • Benjamin Godard (1849-1895): Berceuse from the Opera "Jocelyn" (parts)
    • Gustav Langer (1843-1877): Großmütterchen (parts)
    • Offenbach: Barcarolle from the Opera "Hoffmanns Erzählungen" (parts)
    • Carl Zeller (1842-1898): Sei nicht bös! aus der Operette "Der Obersteiger" (parts)
    • Carl Zeller: Adam Walzer aus der Operette "Der Vogelhändler" (parts)

  • Simone Salvetti (1870 – 1932)
    • Fra l'ombre della Pineta (score)
    • Mormorio del Mare (parts)
    • Poesia Alpestre (parts)
    • Rapsodia Spagnole (score)
    • Sogno Bizzarro (score)
    • Tempi che furone (score)

  • Giacomo Sartori (1860 - 1946)
    • Impressioni Musicali (score)
    • Le Campane di S. Lucio (parts)
    • Nostalgia del passato (score)
    • Tramonto (score and parts edited by U. Priss) (Played here by the Braunschweig Mandolin Orchestra.)
    • En giro al sass (parts)
    • Other works by Sartori are available here.

  • Otto Schick (1850 - 1928)
    • El Jaleo di Xeres (parts)
    • Madrilena (Dance Espagnole) (parts)
    • Mandolinen Mazurka (parts). This piece is dedicated to the Braunschweiger Mandolinenquartett which was not the same as the still existing Braunschweiger Mandolinenverein but one player, Erich Franke, played in both, although at a later time.
    arranged by Schick:
    • Abundio Martinez (1875-1914): Auf hohem Meere (parts)
    • Mendelssohn: Chanson de printemps (parts)
    • Giacomo Meyerbeer (1791-1864): March (parts)
    • Erik Meyer-Helmund (1861-1932): Ballgeflüster (parts)
    • Mozart: Menuett aus der Es-dur Sinfonie (parts)
    • Ferdinand Radeck (1828-1903): Fridericus Rex Grenadier March (parts)
    • Hermann Reh: Gavotte Royale (parts)
    • Rossini: Fantasy over Rossini's La gazza ladra (parts)
    • Strauss: Annen-Polka (parts)
    • Verdi: Fantasy over Verdi's Rigoletto (parts)
    • Verdi: Fantasy over Verdi's Troubadour (parts)
    • Franz Von Blon (1861–1945): Frühlings-Einzug March (parts)
    • Weber: Ouverture of Weber's Oberon (parts)
    Schick's Mandolinenalbum Volume 2 scanned by Google

  • Paul Spiegelberg (1859-1937)
    • Zur Weihe des Tages (parts)

  • Emil Stoye (according to the Köln address book a music teacher who died sometime between 1930 and 1935)
    • Ein Hoch der Zupfmusik (parts)
    arranged by Stoye:
    • Leon Jessel (1871-1942): Der Rose Hochzeitszug (parts)
    • Oskar Fetras (1854-1931): Mondnacht auf der Alster (parts)
    • Rudolf Förster (1860-1894): Mandolinen-Serenade (parts)
    • Juventino Rosas (1868-1894): Über den Wellen (parts)
    • Theodor Rupprecht (1873-1934): Prisca aus der Operette: "Die Najaden" (parts)

  • Willy Volmert (according to the Düsseldorf address book a pseudonym of Josef Volmert who worked as a master craftsman/foreman in a factory and had a small publishing company for his own compositions. He died ca 1935.)
    • Deutscher Mandolinistenmarsch (parts)

  • Henry Wormsbacher (1866 - 1934)
    • Freiheitsgeist - Spirit of Liberty March (parts)
    • Herzlich Willkommen (parts)
    • Serenade - Pathetique! (parts)
    • Volkslieder Ranken (parts)
    arranged by Wormsbacher:
    • Johann Schrammel (1850-1893): Wien bleibt Wien (parts)

For mandolin and guitar

 From the collection of Willi Lorenz, a former member of the Braunschweig Mandolin Orchestra.
  • Vincenzo Arienzo (1873 - 1907, presumably a pseudonym of Enrico Marucelli)
    • 2nd Capriccio di Concerto (2 parts)

  • Salvator Leonardi (1872 - 1938)
    • La Scintilla (Mazurka) (2 parts)
    • Souvenir de Naples (1 part)
    • Souvenir de Sicile (2 parts)

  • Alfred Heinrich Loreti (1870 – 1944)
    • Zaira (Mazurka) (1 part)

  • Hermyn Siegmund (1863 - 1941, first name also spelled: Hermin. Many modern sources claim that this was a woman. Maybe because the female name "Hermine" is much more common than the male "Hermin". But according to older issues of a guitar magazine on www.digitalguitararchive.com, this was a man who published sheetmusic in his own publishing company in Silesia.)
    • Rondino concertante (2 parts)

Footnote: I am providing the music on this site as a historical record. Some of the composers and some of the music titles and themes are inappropriate from a modern perspective because of a discriminatory nature or because of political views that I do not condone.
